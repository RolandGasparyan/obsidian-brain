## 1. Introduction to Algorithmic Crypto Trading

Cryptocurrency markets operate continuously, 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, with volatility profiles that differ fundamentally from traditional equity markets. The absence of daily session boundaries, the prevalence of highly leveraged participants, and the dominance of algorithmic actors create a market microstructure that rewards speed, precision, and systematic discipline above all else.

The data presented in this encyclopedia is not theoretical. It is derived from a production-grade AI trading system — the Trading Guru Engine — that has executed over 340,000 paper trades on live OKX market data. Every profit factor, win rate, and performance metric cited herein reflects real market conditions, real bid-ask spreads, and real execution latency.

The central thesis of this work is that profitability in algorithmic crypto trading is achieved not by finding a single "holy grail" indicator, but by the systematic combination of three elements: (1) empirically validated signal generation, (2) intelligent position sizing, and (3) ruthless risk management. Each section of this encyclopedia addresses one or more of these pillars.

### 3.1 Chande Momentum Oscillator (CMO)

**Category:** Momentum Capture | **Empirical PF:** 1.278 | **Total PnL:** +$3,529

The Chande Momentum Oscillator is the single highest-performing strategy in the entire engine. It was developed by Tushar Chande and published in his 1994 book *The New Technical Trader*. Unlike the Relative Strength Index (RSI), which only considers gains in its denominator, the CMO utilizes both gains and losses, making it significantly more sensitive to momentum shifts in highly volatile markets.

**Mathematical Formula:**
```
Period = 14
Up Moves  = Sum of max(Close[i] - Close[i-1], 0) for i in last 14 periods
Down Moves = Sum of max(Close[i-1] - Close[i], 0) for i in last 14 periods
CMO = 100 × (Up Moves - Down Moves) / (Up Moves + Down Moves)
Range: -100 to +100
```

**Entry Signal (Short):** CMO(14) > 50 indicates extreme overbought momentum. The engine requires a minimum confirmation score of 2 before opening a position.

**Confirmation Scoring System:**
- CMO > 50: +3 confirms (primary trigger)
- CMO > 30: +1 confirm (weak trigger)
- RSI(14) > 60: +1 confirm
- MACD histogram < 0: +1 confirm
- Price > EMA(20): +1 confirm
- Volume > 120% of 10-period SMA: +1 confirm

**Stop Loss:** 1.5× ATR(14) above entry price.
**Take Profit:** 2.5× the stop loss distance (2.5:1 Risk-Reward Ratio).
**Exit Method:** 98.8% of trades exit via timeout (8-12 ticks). The strategy is a pure momentum-capture system.

### 3.2 ICT Fair Value Gap (FVG)

**Category:** Smart Money Concepts | **Empirical PF:** 1.121 | **Total PnL:** +$1,344

The Fair Value Gap is a concept from the Inner Circle Trader (ICT) methodology. It identifies a three-candle pattern where a rapid, impulsive price move creates a price imbalance — a gap between the high of the first candle and the low of the third candle. The market has a strong statistical tendency to retrace into this gap before resuming the original trend, as institutional algorithms seek to fill the inefficiency.

**Pattern Definition (Bearish FVG):**
```
Candle[i-2].High < Candle[i].Low  → Bullish FVG (price gap upward)
Candle[i-2].High > Candle[i].Low  → Bearish FVG (price gap downward)
Minimum gap size: 0.1% of price
```

**Entry Signal (Short):** A bearish FVG forms. Price subsequently retraces upward into the gap zone and shows rejection (a bearish close within the gap).

**Confirmation:** RSI(14) > 55, price above 20-period EMA.
**Stop Loss:** Above the top of the FVG zone + 0.5× ATR.
**Take Profit:** 2.5× the stop loss distance.

**Best Performing Pairs:** BOME/USDT (PF 4.28), OP/USDT (PF 2.78), ADA/USDT (PF 2.23).

### 3.3 Squeeze Momentum (LazyBear)

**Category:** Volatility Breakout | **Empirical PF:** 1.082 | **Total PnL:** +$1,079

The Squeeze Momentum indicator, popularized by John Carter and implemented by LazyBear on TradingView, identifies periods of extreme low volatility (the "squeeze") that precede explosive breakout moves. It works by detecting when Bollinger Bands contract inside Keltner Channels — a condition that historically precedes high-momentum moves.

**Squeeze Detection:**
```
Bollinger Bands (20, 2.0) are INSIDE Keltner Channels (20, 1.5)
→ Squeeze is ON (market coiling)

When Bollinger Bands expand OUTSIDE Keltner Channels
→ Squeeze FIRES (breakout imminent)
```

**Entry Signal (Short):** Squeeze fires AND the momentum histogram is negative (downward momentum).
**Confirmation:** RSI > 55, Volume > 130% of 10-period SMA.
**Stop Loss:** 1.5× ATR above entry.
**Take Profit:** 2.5× stop loss distance.

**Broad Applicability:** This strategy works across all top 8 assets, making it the most universally deployable strategy in the engine.

## 4. Tier 2 Strategies — Asset Specialists

### 4.1 Williams %R Reversal

**Category:** Mean Reversion | **Empirical PF:** 1.069 | **Total PnL:** +$499

Developed by Larry Williams, the Williams Percent Range (%R) measures the current closing price relative to the highest high over a lookback period, expressed as a percentage. Values above -20 indicate overbought conditions.

**Formula:**
```
%R = (Highest High - Close) / (Highest High - Lowest Low) × -100
Range: -100 to 0
Overbought: above -20
Oversold: below -80
```

**Entry Signal (Short):** %R(14) rises above -20 (overbought) and then crosses back below -20 (momentum reversal confirmed).
**Best Pair:** FLOKI/USDT (PF 4.61).

### 4.2 Order Flow Imbalance

**Category:** Market Microstructure | **Empirical PF:** 1.056 | **Total PnL:** +$1,055

Order flow analysis examines the ratio of aggressive sell orders to total volume. When sellers are significantly more aggressive than buyers, price tends to continue declining. This is the most broadly applicable strategy in the engine, performing profitably across all top 10 assets.

**Signal Calculation:**
```
Sell Ratio = Sell Volume / (Buy Volume + Sell Volume)
Imbalance Signal: Sell Ratio > 60% over last 5 candles
```

**Entry Signal (Short):** Sell ratio exceeds 60% with a volume spike above 120% of average.

### 4.3 Keltner Channel + RSI

**Category:** Volatility / Mean Reversion | **Empirical PF:** 1.046 | **Total PnL:** +$837

Keltner Channels use the Average True Range (ATR) to define dynamic support and resistance bands around an Exponential Moving Average. When price closes above the upper band simultaneously with RSI in overbought territory, it signals a high-probability short entry.

**Channel Formula:**
```
Middle Line = EMA(20)
Upper Band  = EMA(20) + 2.0 × ATR(14)
Lower Band  = EMA(20) - 2.0 × ATR(14)
```

**Entry Signal (Short):** Price closes above the upper Keltner Channel AND RSI(14) > 70.
**Best Pair:** OP/USDT (PF 2.39).

### 4.4 CVD Absorption Reversal

**Category:** Volume / Microstructure | **Empirical PF:** 1.042 | **Total PnL:** +$168

Cumulative Volume Delta (CVD) tracks the running total of the difference between buying volume (aggressive market buys) and selling volume (aggressive market sells). A rising CVD indicates buyers are dominant. However, when CVD rises but price fails to make higher highs, it signals that sellers are *absorbing* the buying pressure — a powerful bearish reversal signal.

**Absorption Detection:**
```
CVD rising over last N candles (buyers dominant)
AND price is flat or declining
→ Seller Absorption confirmed
Absorption Ratio = (CVD Change) / (Price Change) > 0.7
```

### 4.5 EMA 9/21 Crossover

**Category:** Trend Following | **Empirical PF:** 1.054 | **Total PnL:** +$103

The dual EMA crossover is one of the oldest and most widely used trend-following indicators. The 9-period EMA serves as the fast line and the 21-period EMA as the slow line. When the fast line crosses below the slow line (a "Death Cross" on short timeframes), it signals the beginning of a downtrend.

**Entry Signal (Short):** EMA(9) crosses below EMA(21). Confirmed by price below EMA(50) and RSI < 50.

### 4.6 Parabolic SAR Flip

**Category:** Trend Reversal | **Empirical PF:** 1.008 (overall) | **Best Pair PF:** 4.16 (WIF)

The Parabolic Stop and Reverse (SAR) indicator places dots above or below price to indicate trend direction. When the SAR flips from below price to above price, it signals a trend reversal to the downside.

**Formula:**
```
SAR = Previous SAR + AF × (EP - Previous SAR)
AF = Acceleration Factor (starts at 0.02, increases by 0.02 each period, max 0.2)
EP = Extreme Point (highest high in current trend)
```

**Secret:** While the overall PF is modest (1.008), this strategy has an exceptional PF of 4.16 on WIF/USDT and 3.05 on FLOKI/USDT. It should be deployed exclusively on these assets.

### 4.7 Bollinger Band Squeeze

**Category:** Volatility Breakout | **Empirical PF:** 1.010 (overall) | **Best Pair PF:** 2.37 (DOGE)

Bollinger Bands contract during periods of low volatility and expand during high volatility. A "squeeze" occurs when the bands narrow to their tightest point in a defined lookback period, signaling an impending breakout.

**Squeeze Detection:**
```
Band Width = (Upper Band - Lower Band) / Middle Band
Squeeze: Band Width at 6-month low
```

Entry Signal: Z-Score > 2.0 (price is 2 standard deviations above mean)

**Category:** Trend Strength | **Role:** Filter (not standalone)

### 5.1 ALMA + Stochastic Reversal (PF: 0.984)

### 9.1 The Volatility Asymmetry Secret

The most important discovery in the dataset is the **Volatility Asymmetry** principle: high-volatility assets (FLOKI, BOME, BONK) generate disproportionately high Profit Factors because their large ATR% means winning trades capture large moves while losing trades are cut by the same ATR-based stop loss.

The formula is:
```
Expected Edge = (Win Rate × Avg Win) - (Loss Rate × Avg Loss)
For CMO on FLOKI: (0.245 × $5.80) - (0.755 × $0.37) = +$1.14 per trade
For CMO on BTC:   (0.481 × $1.20) - (0.519 × $1.22) = -$0.06 per trade
```

The difference is entirely explained by the ATR% of each asset.

## 10. Position Sizing Systems

### 10.1 The Gods Level Position Sizing Formula

The foundation of all position sizing in the Trading Guru Engine is the Gods Level formula, derived from the Kelly Criterion:

```
RISK = Capital × 0.02 (2% of capital per trade)
STOP_PCT = ATR(14) / Current Price × 1.5
POSITION = RISK / STOP_PCT
KELLY_ADJUSTED = POSITION × 0.25 (Kelly multiplier for safety)
FINAL_POSITION = min(KELLY_ADJUSTED, Capital × 0.08) (8% hard cap)
```

This formula ensures that position size automatically adjusts to market volatility — larger positions in low-volatility environments and smaller positions when volatility spikes.

### 10.2 Fixed Fractional Position Sizing

The simplest and most widely used position sizing method. A fixed percentage of capital is risked on every trade.

```
Position Size = Capital × Risk% / Stop Loss%
Example: $10,000 × 2% / 1.5% = $1,333 position
```

### 10.3 Kelly Criterion

The Kelly Criterion calculates the theoretically optimal fraction of capital to bet to maximize long-term growth:

```
f* = (Win Rate × Avg Win - Loss Rate × Avg Loss) / Avg Win
For CMO: f* = (0.448 × 1.39 - 0.552 × 0.88) / 1.39 = 0.097 (9.7%)
```

The engine uses a 0.25 Kelly multiplier (25% of full Kelly) for safety, resulting in a ~2.4% position size.

### 10.4 Volatility-Adjusted Sizing

Position size is inversely proportional to ATR:

```
Target Risk = Capital × 2%
ATR_Stop = ATR(14) × 1.5
Position = Target Risk / ATR_Stop
```

When ATR is high (volatile market), position size decreases automatically. When ATR is low (quiet market), position size increases.

## 11. Anti-Martingale: The Gods Level Sizing System

### 11.1 The Problem with Martingale

The traditional Martingale system doubles position size after every loss to recover capital. In the Trading Guru Engine's legacy "Smart Doubling" system, this resulted in:

- Overall Profit Factor: **1.037** (barely profitable)

### 11.2 The Anti-Martingale Solution

The Anti-Martingale system reverses the logic: it increases position size after **wins** and resets to the base size after **losses**. This capitalizes on winning streaks while protecting capital during losing streaks.

**Multiplier Sequence:**
```
Win Streak = 0 → Position Multiplier = 1x (base)
Win Streak = 1 → Position Multiplier = 2x
Win Streak = 2 → Position Multiplier = 4x
Win Streak = 3 → Position Multiplier = 8x (maximum)
Any LOSS       → Win Streak resets to 0 (back to 1x)
Hard Cap: Position cannot exceed 8% of capital
```

### 11.3 Why Anti-Martingale Works in Crypto

Cryptocurrency markets exhibit **momentum clustering** — winning trades tend to cluster together during trending conditions, and losing trades cluster during choppy conditions. The Anti-Martingale system naturally amplifies capital during favorable trending conditions (when win streaks occur) and minimizes exposure during unfavorable conditions (when losses reset the streak).

## 13. Risk Management Framework

### 13.1 The Three Laws of Crypto Risk Management

**Law 1: Never Risk More Than 2% Per Trade.**
The 2% rule ensures that even a 10-trade losing streak (statistically rare) only reduces capital by approximately 18%, leaving the system fully operational.

**Law 2: Always Use ATR-Based Stops.**
Fixed percentage stops ignore market volatility. An ATR-based stop automatically widens during volatile periods (avoiding premature stop-outs) and tightens during calm periods (protecting profits).

**Law 3: Maintain a Minimum 2:1 Risk-Reward Ratio.**
With a 45% win rate, a 2:1 R:R ratio produces a positive expectancy of approximately +0.35% per trade. Below 2:1, positive expectancy requires a win rate above 50%.

**Mathematical Proof:**
```
Expectancy = (Win Rate × Reward) - (Loss Rate × Risk)
At 45% WR, 2:1 R:R: (0.45 × 2) - (0.55 × 1) = +0.35 (positive)
At 45% WR, 1:1 R:R: (0.45 × 1) - (0.55 × 1) = -0.10 (negative)
```

### 13.2 Maximum Drawdown Management

The engine tracks maximum drawdown in real time and applies progressive risk reduction:

| Drawdown Level | Action |
|---------------|--------|
| < 5% | Normal operation |
| 5–10% | Reduce position size by 25% |
| 10–15% | Reduce position size by 50% |
| > 15% | Pause all trading for 24 hours |

### 13.3 Correlation Risk

Trading multiple assets simultaneously creates correlation risk — if all assets decline together, multiple positions lose simultaneously. The engine limits this by:
- Maximum 3 concurrent open positions

- Avoiding opening new positions when 2+ existing positions are in drawdown

## 14. On-Chain Intelligence Layer

On-chain data provides insights into the behavior of large market participants ("whales") that are invisible in price charts alone.

### 14.1 MVRV Z-Score

The Market Value to Realized Value (MVRV) Z-Score measures whether an asset is overvalued or undervalued relative to its "fair value" (the average price at which all coins were last moved).

```
MVRV Ratio = Market Cap / Realized Cap
Z-Score = (Market Cap - Realized Cap) / Standard Deviation
```

**Trading Signal:**
- Z-Score > 2.5: Asset significantly overvalued → reduce position size by 50%
- Z-Score < 0: Asset undervalued → increase position size by 25%

### 14.2 Exchange Inflow Spikes

When large amounts of cryptocurrency flow into exchanges, it typically signals that large holders intend to sell. A sudden spike in exchange inflows (> 3× the 30-day average) is a bearish signal.

**Action:** Skip new long entries for 1–2 hours after an exchange inflow spike.

### 14.3 Funding Rate Analysis

In perpetual futures markets, the funding rate is the periodic payment between long and short position holders. When the funding rate is highly positive (> 0.1%), the market is over-leveraged long, and a short squeeze is less likely.

**Action:** When funding rate > 0.1%, increase confidence score for short entries by +1.

## 15. Market Microstructure Secrets

### 15.1 The Liquidity Sweep Pattern

One of the most powerful and consistently profitable patterns in crypto markets is the liquidity sweep. Large institutional players deliberately push price above a significant swing high to trigger retail stop-losses (which are buy orders), creating the liquidity needed to fill their large short positions. The sequence is:

1. Price consolidates below a significant swing high.
2. Price briefly spikes above the swing high (the "sweep").
3. Price immediately reverses and closes back below the swing high.
4. A short position is entered on the close of the reversal candle.

This pattern is the foundation of the ICT Order Block + Liquidity Sweep strategy (PF 1.017 overall, PF 2.71 on PEPE).

### 15.2 The Fair Value Gap Magnet Effect

Price has a strong statistical tendency to return to fill Fair Value Gaps (FVGs). When a rapid impulsive move creates a gap, it acts as a "magnet" that attracts price back to the gap zone. The ICT FVG strategy (PF 1.121) exploits this tendency by entering short positions when price retraces into a bearish FVG.

### 15.3 The Volume Profile Secret

Volume Profile analysis reveals the price levels where the most trading volume has occurred historically. These "high volume nodes" act as strong support and resistance levels. The engine uses the Point of Control (POC) — the price level with the highest volume — as a key reference point for stop placement.

### 15.4 The ATR Expansion Pattern

When ATR suddenly expands to 2× its 20-period average, it signals that a significant directional move is underway. Entering in the direction of the expansion (short if price is declining) and holding until ATR contracts back to normal provides a high-probability trade.
